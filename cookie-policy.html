Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how First in Dallas ("we," "our," or "us") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website and in our newsletter services. By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy. This policy should be read in conjunction with our Privacy Policy.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet, or other electronic device) when you visit a website. Cookies allow websites to recognize your device, remember your preferences, and provide a more personalized user experience. Cookies can be "persistent" (remaining on your device until deleted or expired) or "session" (deleted when you close your browser).

Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies for the following purposes:

  • Essential Functionality: To enable core website features and ensure proper operation of our services
  • Analytics and Performance: To understand how visitors interact with our website and newsletter, including page views, traffic sources, and user behavior
  • Personalization: To remember your preferences and provide a customized experience
  • Newsletter Engagement: To track email open rates, click-through rates, and content engagement
  • Advertising and Marketing: To deliver relevant advertisements and measure the effectiveness of marketing campaigns
  • Security: To protect against fraud, unauthorized access, and security threats

Types of Cookies We Use

1. Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for our website to function properly. They enable basic features such as page navigation, access to secure areas, and form submissions. Without these cookies, certain services cannot be provided.

Examples: Session management, security tokens, load balancing

Duration: Session or up to 1 year

2. Analytics and Performance Cookies

These cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, such as which pages are visited most often, how long users stay on pages, and whether they encounter error messages. This data helps us improve our website's performance and user experience.

Examples: Google Analytics, Beehiiv analytics

Duration: Up to 2 years

Data Collected: Page views, bounce rate, session duration, traffic sources, device information

3. Functionality Cookies

These cookies allow our website to remember choices you make (such as your language preference or region) and provide enhanced, personalized features. They may also be used to provide services you have requested, such as watching a video or commenting on content.

Examples: User preferences, language settings, saved content

Duration: Session or up to 1 year

4. Advertising and Marketing Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver advertisements that are relevant to you and your interests. They may also be used to limit the number of times you see an advertisement and measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns. These cookies are often placed by third-party advertising networks with our permission.

Examples: Ad targeting, retargeting, conversion tracking

Duration: Up to 2 years

Third Parties: May include advertising networks and social media platforms

Newsletter Tracking

When you subscribe to our newsletter, we use tracking technologies to measure engagement and improve our content. This includes:

  • Email Open Tracking: We use invisible tracking pixels to determine if and when you open our emails
  • Click Tracking: We track which links you click within our newsletter to understand content preferences

  • Engagement Metrics: We analyze how long you spend reading our content and which sections are most engaging

This data helps us deliver more relevant content and improve the overall newsletter experience. You can opt out of email tracking by disabling images in your email client or unsubscribing from our newsletter.

How to Manage and Disable Cookies

You have the right to control and manage cookies. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can modify your browser settings to decline cookies or alert you when cookies are being sent. Please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of our website and limit your ability to use certain features.

Browser Settings

Opt-Out Tools

You can opt out of certain types of cookies and tracking:

  • Google Analytics: Use the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Advertising Cookies: Visit the Digital Advertising Alliance or Network Advertising Initiative to opt out of interest-based advertising
  • Do Not Track: Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal, though not all websites honor this signal

Mobile Device Tracking

If you access our website or newsletter via a mobile device, you may be able to control tracking through your device settings. Most mobile operating systems (iOS, Android) provide settings to limit ad tracking and reset advertising identifiers. Consult your device's help documentation for specific instructions.

Data Retention

Cookie data is retained for varying periods depending on the type of cookie and its purpose. Session cookies are deleted when you close your browser, while persistent cookies remain on your device until they expire or you delete them. Analytics data collected through cookies is typically retained for up to 26 months, in accordance with industry standards and legal requirements.
